GENRE

Genre's that you must like

OPM

OPM is Original Pilipino Music, initially referring to Filipino pop ballads in the late 1970s and during 1980s. Nowadays, the term is used to categorize all popular music that is composed and performed by Filipino or originating from the country.

K-POP

K-pop or Korean Pop is the internationally popular, aesthetic-driven, style-bending, trendsetting, music genre of the 21st century. Originating in South Korea, K-pop draws influence from a range of genres like pop, experimental, rock, hip-hop, R&B, electronic, and dance.

Worship Music

Worship music is praise. The majority of biblical songs contain elements of praise, if it is not their primary theme. Praise-songs include several genres: psalms, canticles, hymns, and doxologies. The psalms were sung in the OT in the synagogue and the early church kept this practice. But the Word of God also commands the church to sing “new songs” (Ps. 149:1).

OPM

ERE by JK Labajo

“ERE” is the 3rd official single from Juan Karlos' album “Sad Songs and Bullshit Part 1” and was released on August 4, 2023. The album was initially comprised of 18 songs but was split up into 2 parts, with Part 2 set to be released in early 2024. The song debuted at the No. 177 spot with 1.22 million streams. It broke the all-time record for the biggest single-day stream on Spotify Philippines with 1.39 million streams, surpassing Taylor Swift’s Mine (Taylor’s Version), making it the Philippines’ most streamed local song in a day this year (2023). “ERE” was also featured on Season 1 Episode 16 entitled “Governor’s Ball”. of the ABS-CBN teleserye “Senior High”, a series that Juan Karlos stars in. In the episode, JK’s character Gino performs the song together with his bandmates in the show. The episode aired on September 18, 2023.

GIVE ME YOUR FOREVER by Zack Tabudlo

‘Give Me Your Forever’ is about true love and how you can show love to your partner in whatever situation. It’s how you can show your forever to the person you love. Whether it be virtually or physically, it’s how you do your ways and how you make them happy and make them feel your love with your own take [on life].




BUMALIK KA NA SAKIN by Silent Santuary

A song about longing for someone who has left and wanting them to come back. The lyrics express the narrator's desire for the person they love to return and reconcile with them. The song is filled with emotions of regret, hope, and the yearning for a second chance in the relationship.




RAINING IN MANILA by Lola Amour

The song Raining In Manila by Lola Amour reveals a story of longing, and hope through the experiences of a person caught in the rain in the city of Manila. The song speaks of a person who is missing someone deeply, but cannot communicate with them due to the weather or distance. Overall, Raining In Manila expresses the sadness of missing someone and the feeling of being powerless when communication is difficult. The rainy weather serves as a metaphor for the stormy emotions that come with heartbreak, but also as a reminder that the storms will eventually pass, and brighter days will come.

Biyahe by Jroa

A song about how the protagonist tells his partner to hold his hand and he will take her wherever she wants to go. He promises to be her guide and to stay by her side no matter what happens, and they will go on this journey together. He reassures her that he will never let go of her hand, and they can face anything that comes their way.




K-POP

TT by Twice

"TT" is a song recorded by South Korean girl group Twice. The song was released by JYP Entertainment on October 24, 2016, as the lead single from their third extended play Twicecoaster: Lane 1. It was written and composed by Sam Lewis and Black Eyed Pilseung respectively. The title "TT" refers to an emoticon used to express crying or sadness.





GROWL by Exo

"Growl" (Korean: 으르렁 Eureureong; Chinese: 咆哮 Páoxiào) is a song recorded by South Korean–Chinese boy band Exo, released on August 5, 2013, for the repackaged edition of their first studio album XOXO. It was released in both Korean and Chinese versions by their label SM Entertainment. The song is known as Exo's breakthrough single, with over two million downloads in South Korea.



DON'T WANNA CRY by Seventeen

"Don't Wanna Cry" (Korean: 울고 싶지 않아) is a song released by South Korean boy band Seventeen on May 22, 2017, as the lead single from their EP Al1. "Don't Wanna Cry" is a major departure from the group's previous singles, which consisted mainly of lighthearted, upbeat love songs. The lyrics describe post-breakup feelings of heartbreak and loneliness. Additionally, it incorporated elements of EDM and electropop and was less influenced by hip hop than their previous work.

LA VI EN ROSE by Iz*One

"La Vie en Rose" (Korean: 라비앙로즈; RR: Rabiangrojeu) is the debut single by South Korean–Japanese girl group Iz*One, released on October 29, 2018 by Off the Record Entertainment as the lead single from their debut extended play (EP) Color*Iz. It was written, composed and produced by the MosPick Music Producing Group from Cube Entertainment.



'I AM' by Ive

"I Am" is a song recorded by South Korean girl group Ive for their first studio album I've Ive. It was released as the album's lead single by Starship Entertainment on April 10, 2023. Its lyrics were written by Kim Eana whilst composition was handled by Ryan S. Jhun, Kristin Marie Skolem, Audun Agnar Guldbrandsen, and Eline Noelia Myreng. The song reached number one on the Circle Digital Chart, becoming Ive's fourth number-one single on the chart.


Worship Songs

“10,000 Reasons (Bless the Lord)” by Matt Redman and Passion

This song describes how there are more than enough reasons for us to worship God, and it names many of the reasons in its multiple verses. It's also ideal for large crowds, as the song is slow and easy to learn.






“Cornerstone” by Hillsong Worship

Hillsong Worship is arguably one of the most well-known Christian worship groups. The band has given us more than a handful of excellent songs like “Cornerstone” – an anthem emphasizing God’s sovereignty and glory.





“What a Beautiful Name” by Hillsong Worship

Hillsong Worship also gave us the song "What a Beautiful Name," which talks about all the goodness and grace God has showered upon us. It also expounds on God's character and how Christians should respond in great worship and gratefulness.




“Amazing Grace (My Chains are Gone)” by Chris Tomlin

Chris Tomlin gives us a twist on the classic gospel song “Amazing Grace”, empowering both young and old church people to find insight and inspiration in God’s grace. The song talks about how God came to save everybody from eternal death, so Christians may find everlasting life with Him.


"This is Amazing Grace" by Phil Wickham

Another praise song about grace is "This is Amazing Grace" by Phil Wickham. While the premise is similar to the earlier piece, this one is more lively and can get the congregation jumping. The song talks about how powerful God is, reminds us of everything He's done, and encourages all to respond in praise.
